A few days ago, I shared v4.1 of Clarity Coach: four rewrite tones, a "why this works" breakdown, and visual scoring instead of a single number.

Since then, I've shipped the piece that actually makes it a coach.

🔹 Accounts and history. Every message you improve is now saved — not just handed back and forgotten.

🔹 A progress dashboard. Clarity, politeness, and professionalism scores plotted over time, so you can see the trend, not just the moment.

🔹 Coach's Insight. This is the one I care about most. Instead of a generic tip on a single message, Clarity Coach now looks across your recent history and surfaces the pattern underneath it — like consistently under-explaining requests, or a tone that reads more curt than intended. Something no single-message tool would ever catch.

The thesis still hasn't changed: correction happens once. Coaching happens over time. A tool that only ever sees one message at a time can't tell you what you keep doing — only what's wrong right now.

Still early, still building. Curious from anyone managing global or multilingual teams: does seeing your own communication patterns over time change how you'd actually use a tool like this?
